Saberes Early Education: A fantasy with imagination and affection

Saberes focuses on the training of educational agents (preschool teachers or “community mothers”), contributing to the comprehensive and quality care of boys and girls in their early childhood, meaning, until the 5th year of age.

In Colombia, early education is a part of the Early Childhood public policy, guaranteeing the compliance of the rights of children. The “community mothers” work in care centers provided by the national government though the Colombian Institute of Family Welfare (ICBF, due to its acronym in Spanish). With this project, the Carvajal Foundation Works in line with the public policies of the national government and the Sustainable Development Objectives of the United Nations, contributing to the improvement of the quality of early education in Colombia.

To exercise this importance role in society, the Carvajal Foundation supports the training of the “community mothers” through a space for training, professionalization and support for 300 hours, and the supply of the educational materials for the “community mothers”, helping them become early childhood educators. As an additional benefit, Saberes strengthens the family ties.

Saberes has trained and supported 1,713 educational agents or “community mothers” who serve 18,712 boys and girls per year in vulnerable areas in Colombia.
